Research

I am a Ph.D. student at Keio University, passionately engaged in research on 3D scene capturing using computer vision and augmented reality techniques. My previous work spans various aspects of 3D view synthesis, including multi-view image capturing with error visualization support in Multi-Plane Image (MPI) and object tracking using neural radiance fields (NeRF). My contributions to the field have been recognized through publications at domestic research conferences.

Throughout my academic journey, I have actively participated in research and internships, all aimed at advancing 3D view synthesis. I am dedicated to capturing reality through User-in-the-loop 3D scene photography to advance digital archiving, with the aim of preserving historical heritage, cultural assets, and documenting disasters.
